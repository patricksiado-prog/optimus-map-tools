# Keep vs Drive — Where Notes Actually Live

## The trap
Searching "keep" inside Google Drive returns junk (WhatsApp chat exports, random files).
Google Keep notes are NOT stored as files in Drive. They live in a separate Keep service.

## What works
- **Direct URL on laptop:** keep.google.com — all notes show up instantly
- **Drive sidebar shortcut:** open drive.google.com → look at the thin vertical strip on the
  right side of the screen → yellow lightbulb icon = Keep panel (notes appear inline)
- **Android:** install Google Keep app → write note → syncs to keep.google.com in seconds

## Workflow for "send myself a Sheets link from phone to laptop"
1. Phone: open Keep, paste Sheets link, save
2. Laptop: keep.google.com — link is there, click it
No Drive round-trip, no email, no copy-paste through messaging apps.

## Alternatives if Keep isn't installed
- **Google Doc** — paste link, close, open from Drive on laptop (slower, but lives in Drive)
- **Telegram Saved Messages** or **WhatsApp "message yourself"** — fastest pure-link transfer

## Why this matters operationally
For pushing Sheets links / GHL contact list URLs / dashboards between Android phone and
laptop during the workday, Keep is the right tool. Don't waste time searching Drive for
Keep content — it will never appear there.
